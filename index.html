Source 1 — Science Article
Teeth vs. Tentacles
By Phillip Jones

1Among fish, great white sharks are the largest predators on the planet. They weigh up to 5,000 pounds and can grow longer than 20 feet. With their streamlined bodies and powerful tails, great white sharks are built for speed. Swimming up to 35 miles per hour, they slice through the ocean like a hot knife through butter. As they bear down on their prey, great white sharks can use their powerful muscles to leap out of the water.

2Scientists think that great white sharks are intelligent hunters. The animals learn quickly, and they cooperate and hunt in groups. A good brain is essential, considering that sharks hunt dolphins, seals, and other cunning animals.

3Great white sharks also are equipped with many senses. Their eyes provide acute vision even in very low light, while their sensitive ears can detect a tiny vibration in the water made by the movement of prey. A great white shark's sense of smell is legendary. Great whites can sniff out a single drop of blood floating in 10 billion drops of water (Furstinger, 2013). Sharks have another important sense: they can detect the direction and strength of electrical currents generated by nearby prey as it moves.

Great white sharks have many rows of teeth — about 300 in total. The rows in the back replace teeth that are worn down or broken. Their bite pressure is 4,000 pounds per inch.

4The giant squid is another ferocious predator. The largest giant squid had a length of about 43 feet and might have weighed about a ton. More typically, giant squids mature to 33 feet long and weigh about 450 pounds. A giant squid possesses the largest eyes in the animal kingdom, measuring about one foot in diameter. The huge eyes enable the squid to distinguish prey in the dark.

5A giant squid can capture prey up to 33 feet away by propelling two feeding tentacles that have powerful suckers ringed with sharp, serrated edges. The tentacles transfer the prey to eight arms covered with toothed suckers. Then, the arms transport the prey to a parrot-like beak, located between the eight arms and tentacles. The squid uses the beak to kill prey and dismember it. The deadly beak is strong enough to bite through steel cable.

Giant squids sneak up on their prey or chase it. Their tentacles wrap around their prey to keep it from getting away. Using their beaks, they tear apart and eat their food.
